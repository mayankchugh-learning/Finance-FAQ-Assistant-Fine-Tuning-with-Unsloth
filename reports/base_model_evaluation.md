**Model:** `unsloth/Qwen2.5-0.5B-Instruct` (no fine-tuning)
**Domain:** Finance FAQ Assistant
**Purpose:** Establish a "before" baseline so we can measure the impact of non-instruction fine-tuning, instruction fine-tuning, and DPO alignment.

## Summary Observations

- The base model tends to produce **generic, textbook-style answers** rather than the concise, direct, domain-grounded responses expected of an internal finance assistant.
- **Three outright factual errors** were observed in this run: Q2 (implies credit cards don't accrue interest), Q5 (invents a "CVR" term and states an inverted utilization guideline), and Q10 (fabricates "Total Direct Taxes" as the TDS expansion). These aren't just style problems — they're wrong answers a real user could act on.
- Two questions (Q1, Q3) open with an unnecessary AI-disclaimer hedge before getting to any content, which an internal domain assistant shouldn't need.
- Several answers were cut off mid-sentence by the 120-token generation limit, compounding the verbosity problem — the model spends its token budget on preamble rather than the answer.
- This baseline motivates Stage 1 (non-instruction fine-tuning for domain vocabulary) and Stage 2 (instruction fine-tuning for direct, correct Q&A behavior).
