# Final Evaluation — Base vs. SFT vs. DPO-Aligned Model

**Domain:** Finance FAQ Assistant
**Base model:** `unsloth/Qwen2.5-0.5B-Instruct`
**SFT model:** Base + Stage 1 (non-instruction FT) + Stage 2 (instruction FT on 104 examples)
**DPO model:** SFT model + Stage 3 (DPO alignment on 51 preference pairs)

> ⚠️ **Read this before the table.** The template's pre-filled "Best Answer" column assumed DPO would win or tie on nearly every question, based on what the preference dataset was designed to reward. Checked against what `base_model_answers.json`, `sft_model_answers.json`, and `dpo_model_answers.json` **actually contain in this run**, that assumption doesn't hold uniformly. **SFT and DPO give word-for-word identical lead answers on 7 of the 10 questions** — including Q4 ("What is a SIP?"), where both give the *same incorrect* definition ("Simple Account Payment" instead of Systematic Investment Plan). DPO only diverges from SFT's wording on 3 questions, and is more correct/complete on 2 of those (Q2, Q8). There is no DPO regression in this run, but there's also no broad DPO improvement — it's a narrower, more modest effect than the training design intended, and that's the honest finding this table reflects.
>
> As in the other two reports, every SFT/DPO lead answer degenerates into repeated tokens after 1-2 sentences in the raw output — shown here truncated to the coherent part; see the JSON files for full raw text.

## Evaluation Criteria

- **Correctness** — factually accurate financial information
- **Helpfulness** — gives the user an actionable, complete answer
- **Domain accuracy** — correct use of finance terminology
- **Safety** — no harmful, dismissive, or risky financial guidance (e.g., "just ignore it")
- **Tone** — professional, calm, appropriately cautious where needed (e.g., recommending a financial advisor for retirement planning)
- **Clarity** — easy to read, not overly verbose
- **Hallucination reduction** — does not invent numbers, policies, or procedures
- **Professional response quality** — reads like a real internal assistant, not a generic chatbot

## Summary Observations

1. **Base → SFT** improvement is real and consistent: SFT drops AI-disclaimer hedges, answers more directly, and avoids at least 2 of base's 3 outright factual errors (Q2, Q10) — though it inherits base's confusion on Q5 in a milder form and doesn't fix Q8.
2. **SFT → DPO is a much narrower effect than the training design intended.** DPO's output is byte-for-byte identical to SFT's on 7 of 10 questions. Where it does differ, DPO is better on both Q2 and Q8 — genuine, if modest, wins — and only marginally different (not better or worse) on Q10.
3. **No DPO regression was found in this run** — this corrects the "DPO hallucinated a worse SIP answer than SFT" finding used in earlier video/demo content for this project. In this execution, SFT is equally wrong on that question, not correct. **If any existing video, blog post, or demo slides for this project state that SFT got Q4 right and DPO got it wrong, that claim is now out of date relative to these notebooks and should be corrected or re-verified against a fresh run before being repeated.**
4. **Two weaknesses persist through all three stages, unfixed by fine-tuning:** the SIP definition (Q4) and mistaking "why improve your credit score" for "how to improve it" (Q9). Both look like training-data coverage gaps rather than something more fine-tuning epochs would fix — worth checking `instruction_dataset.jsonl` and `preference_dataset.jsonl` for on-topic examples before the next run.
5. Every fine-tuned answer, in this specific evaluation setup, degenerates into repeated tokens beyond the first 1-2 sentences — a decoding-configuration issue (`do_sample=False`, no `repetition_penalty`, no EOS-aware stop) documented in `fine_tuning_explanation.md`, not a fine-tuning-quality issue.
6. The DPO-aligned model is still only as good as the ~104 instruction examples and 51 preference pairs it saw, and remains a 0.5B-parameter proof-of-concept — not something to use for definitive legal, tax, or investment advice without a human in the loop.
